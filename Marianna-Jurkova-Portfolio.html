About This Project

These photographs explore spaces of gathering—not as portraits of individuals, but as reflections on collective presence. From crowded streets to emptied stadiums, from intimate moments among those closest to us to architectural environments shaped to hold human bodies, each image considers how we inhabit shared space. Water appears as both setting and subject—a surface for quiet reflection, a field of motion and effort, a place where stillness and intensity coexist.

The work is rooted in the idea that significance often resides not in singular moments, but in the spaces between them. Where collective energy emerges, whether visible or latent. These are gathering grounds, both occupied and waiting. Moments of stillness and effort coexist with movement and chaos. Life in all its forms—human presence, fleeting shadows, traces of growth—creates meaning through proximity and shared breath.

Shot across multiple locations and seasons, this body of work reflects an ongoing conversation about how we occupy space together, and how meaning is constructed through presence—our own and others'. Some moments are celebrated, others pass unnoticed. Yet all contain the essence of the space between people. Of what it means to exist in relation to one another.
